Common Cyber Threats

🎣 Phishing

Fake emails or websites designed to steal passwords and personal information.

🦠 Malware

Viruses and harmful software that can damage your computer or steal your data.

😠 Cyberbullying

Harassing or threatening people through social media, games or messaging apps.

🕵 Identity Theft

Someone using your personal information to pretend to be you.

Cyber Safety Tips

  • ✔ Use strong and unique passwords.
  • ✔ Enable Two-Factor Authentication.
  • ✔ Never share OTPs or passwords.
  • ✔ Keep software updated.
  • ✔ Avoid suspicious links.
  • ✔ Download apps only from trusted sources.
  • ✔ Install antivirus software.
  • ✔ Think before sharing personal information.

Social Media Safety

Keep your account private.
Accept requests only from people you know.
Never share your location publicly.
Report fake or suspicious accounts.

What To Do If You Get Hacked

  1. Change your password immediately.
  2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication.
  3. Scan your device for malware.
  4. Inform your bank if financial data is involved.
  5. Report the cybercrime.

Cyber Safety Facts

📧 Most cyber attacks begin with phishing emails.
🔑 Weak passwords are among the biggest causes of hacked accounts.
📶 Public Wi-Fi can be risky if unsecured.
